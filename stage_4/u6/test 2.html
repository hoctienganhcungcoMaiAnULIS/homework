GRAMMAR CONTEXT: MEMORIES & SCHOOL

Understanding how to talk about the past is a significant accomplishment for any English learner. Often, we talk about actions happening simultaneously. For example, while one person was doing homework, another might have been playing football.

Sometimes, a short action interrupts a longer action. Imagine you were walking to school when you suddenly met your teacher. That moment of meeting stopped the continuous action of walking.

We also use the Present Perfect to talk about experiences without a specific time. Have you ever eaten Sushi? Or have you lived in a town for many years? These structures help us describe our lives vividly.
