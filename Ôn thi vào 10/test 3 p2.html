Part 3: Recycling is the process of turning old or used materials into new products. It is important because it helps protect the environment by reducing the use of natural resources such as trees, water, and metals. When we recycle, we save energy as producing items from recycled materials often requires less energy than making them from raw resources. Recycling also reduces the amount of waste sent to landfills, which take up a lot of space and can harm the environment. Common materials that can be recycled include paper, glass, plastic, and metal. For example, old newspapers can be made into new paper, and empty soda cans can be turned into aluminium products. Since recycling uses fewer resources and less energy, it also helps reduce pollution. Everyone can take part in recycling by sorting waste at home, using recycling bins, and encouraging others to recycle. Schools and communities often organise recycling programmes to raise awareness and make recycling easier. By working together, we can contribute to a cleaner and more sustainable future for generations to come.
